Chinese Tea Eggs

Description

Chinese tea eggs, known as "cha ye dan" (茶叶蛋), are a popular street food and snack in China, celebrated for their unique flavor and visually appealing marbled appearance. These eggs are typically hard-boiled and then cracked slightly to create a network of fine cracks on the shell.

Ingredients

  • Eggs: 6 to 8 large eggs (preferably free-range or organic)
  • Black tea: 2 to 3 bags or 2 tablespoons of loose black tea
  • Soy sauce: 100 to 150 milliliters (about 1/2 to 2/3 cup)
  • Water: enough to cover the eggs in the pot (approximately 500 to 700 milliliters)
  • Spices: star anise, cinnamon stick...

Steps

  1. Hard-boil the eggs and let them cool.
  2. Crack the shells gently to create a network of cracks.
  3. Simmer the eggs in a mixture of water, black tea, soy sauce, and spices for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour, or longer for a stronger flavor.
  4. Let the eggs steep in the liquid for several hours or overnight for the best flavor.
