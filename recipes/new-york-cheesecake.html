New York Cheesecake

Description

New York cheesecake is a rich and creamy dessert that is renowned for its dense texture and smooth flavor. Originating from New York City, this classic cheesecake is typically made with a base of cream cheese, sugar, and eggs, which are blended together to create a velvety filling. The crust is usually made from crushed graham crackers mixed with butter and sugar, providing a sweet and slightly crunchy contrast to the creamy filling.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 200 grams graham cracker crumbs (or digestive biscuits)
  • 100 grams unsalted butter, melted
  • 50 grams granulated sugar

For the Filling:

  • 900 grams cream cheese, softened
  • 200 grams granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 200 grams sour cream (optional, for added creaminess)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional, for a hint of tang)

Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

  • Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F).
  • Mix Crust Ingredients: In a bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and sugar. Mix until well combined.
  • Form the Crust: Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a 23 cm (9-inch) springform pan to create an even layer. Bake for about 10 minutes, then remove and let it cool.

Step 2: Make the Filling

  • Beat Cream Cheese: In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with an electric mixer until smooth and creamy.
  • Add Sugar: Gradually add the granulated sugar and continue to beat until well combined.
  • Incorporate Eggs: Add the eggs one at a time, mixing on low speed after each addition until just combined. Avoid overmixing.
  • Add Flavorings: Mix in the vanilla extract, sour cream, and lemon juice (if using) until smooth.

Step 3: Bake the Cheesecake

  • Pour Filling into Crust: Pour the cream cheese filling over the cooled crust in the springform pan.
  • Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for about 50-60 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.
  • Cool: Turn off the oven and leave the cheesecake inside with the door slightly ajar for about 1 hour to cool gradually. This helps prevent cracking.

Step 4: Chill and Serve

  • Refrigerate: Once cooled, refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.
  • Remove from Pan: Carefully remove the cheesecake from the springform pan.
  • Add Toppings: Top with fresh fruit, fruit compote, or whipped cream if desired.
  • Slice and Enjoy: Cut into slices and serve chilled.
